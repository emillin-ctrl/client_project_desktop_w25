Counseling and Psychological Services (CAPS)

What is CAPS?

CAPS is a mental health service at the University of Michigan dedicated to supporting students' emotional and psychological well-being. It offers a variety of clinical services, including: Group Work Sessions, Workshops, and Crisis support.

How can Group Therapy help you?

  1. Interpersonal Therapy Groups: Improve relationships and communication in a supportive setting.
  2. Coping Skills Groups: Learn mindfulness, emotion regulation, and stress management.
  3. Identity-Based Groups: Examples include SOGI-focused groups for identity exploration and support.
  4. Support Groups: Peer-led spaces like Wolverine Support Network for open sharing.

Crisis Support

CAPS provides immediate support for students experiencing a mental health crisis.

  1. Business Hours: Visit CAPS in person or call their office.
  2. After Hours: Use the CAPS Urgent Support Line via their main number.
  3. Follow Up: Meet with a CAPS counselor or receive referrals for continued care.
